 # RDF Notes Maintenance Community Group Charter

Start Date: September 1, 2025
Last Modified: August 27, 2025

## Goals
The RDF Notes Maintenance Community Group aims to maintain and update Notes originally published by the W3C Semantic Web Interest Group (now closed), ensuring they reflect changes in RDF, SPARQL, SHACL, OWL, and community practices. The group will produce Community Group Reports for *Microdata to RDF – Second Edition*, *vCard Ontology*, *HTML Data Guide*, *VoID Vocabulary*, and *RDF Calendar*.

## Scope of Work
The group will update the following Notes to align with current standards and practices:
- *Microdata to RDF – Second Edition*: Conversion of HTML microdata to RDF.
- *vCard Ontology*: Vocabulary for describing people and organizations.
- *HTML Data Guide*: Best practices for publishing data in HTML.
- *VoID Vocabulary*: Metadata for describing linked datasets.
- *RDF Calendar*: Application of RDF to iCalendar data.

Key use cases include ensuring IRI persistence (e.g., addressing vocab.deri.ie being offline for VoID), compatibility with RDF 1.2 and SPARQL 1.2, and supporting modern Semantic Web applications. The group will produce Community Group Reports under the W3C Document License, with contributions under the W3C Community Contributor License Agreement (CLA).

## Out of Scope
- Developing new specifications beyond updates to the listed Notes.
- Modifying core RDF, SPARQL, SHACL, or OWL standards, which are handled by other groups (e.g., RDF-star Working Group).
- Publishing reports under licenses other than the W3C Document License.

## Deliverables

### Specifications
The group will produce Community Group Reports for:
- *VoID Vocabulary*: Updated to address IRI persistence and align with RDF/SPARQL 1.2 (estimated Q4 2025).
- *Microdata to RDF – Second Edition*: Updated for modern HTML and RDF standards (estimated Q1 2026).
- *vCard Ontology*: Revised for current use cases (estimated Q2 2026).
- *HTML Data Guide*: Updated best practices (estimated Q2 2026).
- *RDF Calendar*: Aligned with RDF 1.2 (estimated Q3 2026).

### Non-Normative Reports
The group may produce use cases, requirements, or white papers to support Note updates, such as documenting VoID IRI solutions or community feedback on RDF standards.

### Test Suites and Other Software
No test suites or software are planned under the current charter.

## Dependencies or Liaisons
- **RDF-star Working Group**: For alignment on RDF 1.2 and SPARQL 1.2 standards.
- **W3C Team**: For coordination on updating original Notes per W3C Process sections 6.2.5 and 6.4.2.
- **External Communities**: Semantic Web community for feedback on Note updates.

## Community and Business Group Process
The group operates under the Community and Business Group Process. Terms in this Charter that conflict with those of the Community and Business Group Process are void.

W3C seeks organizational licensing commitments under the W3C Community Contributor License Agreement (CLA). When people request to participate without representing their organization's legal interests, W3C will approve those requests with the understanding that W3C will seek an organizational commitment under the CLA starting with the individual’s first contribution to a group Deliverable. The W3C Code of Ethics and Professional Conduct applies to participation in this group.

## Work Limited to Charter Scope
The group will not publish Specifications on topics other than those listed under Specifications above. See below for how to modify the charter.

## Contribution Mechanics
Substantive Contributions to Specifications can only be made by Community Group Participants who have agreed to the W3C Community Contributor License Agreement (CLA). Specifications created in the Community Group must use the W3C Document License. All other documents produced by the group should use that License where possible.

Community Group participants agree to make all contributions in the GitHub repo the group is using for the particular document. This may be in the form of a pull request (preferred), by raising an issue, or by adding a comment to an existing issue. All Github repositories attached to the Community Group must contain a copy of the [CONTRIBUTING](https://github.com/w3c/rdf-notes/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md) and [LICENSE](https://github.com/w3c/rdf-notes/blob/main/LICENSE.md) files.

## Transparency
The group will conduct all of its technical work in public. All technical work will occur in its GitHub repositories (https://github.com/w3c/rdf-notes) and not in mailing list discussions. Meetings may be restricted to Community Group participants, but a public summary or minutes must be posted to the group’s public mailing list (public-rdf-maintenance@w3.org) or to a GitHub issue.

## Decision Process
The group will seek to make decisions where there is consensus. The Chair (Felicia Sephodi) assesses consensus after discussion and consideration of different opinions. If consensus isn’t clear, the Chair may issue a Call for Consensus (CfC) with a 7-day online feedback period. Decisions will be recorded in GitHub issues. Participants can earn Committer status through valuable contributions, as common in open source projects.

If substantial disagreement remains, Committers will choose an alternative with substantial support, using a vote if necessary. Individuals who disagree are encouraged to take ownership of an alternative fork to inform the group’s final choice through implementation experience.

Decisions reached at meetings are tentative and recorded in a GitHub issue. Participants may object within 7 days of publication, providing clear technical reasons. The Chair will facilitate discussion to resolve objections per this process, ensuring fairness and respect for consensus.

## Chair Selection
Participants in this group choose their Chair(s) and can replace them at any time using the process described in the Community and Business Group Process. The current Chair is Felicia Sephodi. If 5 participants, no two from the same organization, call for an election, the group will follow the election process outlined in the Community and Business Group Process, consulting the Community Development Lead and using RFC 2777 for tie-breaking.

## Amendments to this Charter
The group can propose an amended charter using the Decision Process above. Adoption requires a 30-day vote with 2/3 of votes cast in favor. The new charter takes effect on the proposed date or 7 days after the vote result, whichever is later. Simple corrections (e.g., deliverable dates) use the group’s decision process.
